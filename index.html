Who I Am

An expert in modelling complex systems, I am a trained physicist with a deep passion for understanding the fundamental principles that drive both biological systems and societal phenomena. My work integrates physics-based modelling, advanced data analysis, and computational techniques to uncover hidden patterns and mechanisms, predict emergent behaviours, and provide actionable insights. With experience spanning theoretical biophysics, statistical modelling, and policy evaluation, I am committed to applying rigorous scientific methods to tackle pressing real-world challenges, from cellular dynamics to large-scale social systems.

Research

My research focuses on modelling cell motility, intracellular self-organization, and out-of-equilibrium dynamics. I also explore applications of theoretical modelling in societal contexts, such as refugee dynamics and education policy.

Projects

  • Out-of-equilibrium molecular self-organisation in the cell
  • Agent-based simulations of migration dynamics
  • Infectious disease modelling for policy
