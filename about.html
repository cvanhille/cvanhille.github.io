Who I am

My research focuses on modelling cell motility, intracellular self-organization, and out-of-equilibrium dynamics. I also explore applications of theoretical modelling in societal contexts, such as refugee dynamics and infectious disease transmission and prevention.

Who I am

With a PhD in Computational and Theoretical Physics and eager to expand the reach of my work beyond academia, I am ready to apply my expertise in advanced mathematical modelling, data analysis and computational methods, along with my extensive scientific experience, to support the development of sophisticated infectious disease models that address transmission and control of vaccine preventable diseases.
